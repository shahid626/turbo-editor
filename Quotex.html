1) Candle Basics & Wick Rules (The core)

Candles are the primary unit. Learn body, open, close, high, low, and wicks.

  • Lower wick long → buyers rejected lower prices → Next candle likely GREEN.
  • Upper wick long → sellers rejected highs → Next candle likely RED.
  • Body vs wick ratio: wick ≥ 2× body = higher probability reversal. 3× = very strong.

Mini-Cheat

Lower wick → GREEN
Upper wick → RED
Double wicks → Strong S/R
Small body + long wick → Smart money trap

Practice

  1. Open 1-minute chart demo mode.
  2. Mark candles with long lower wicks. Watch next candle for 20 examples and record success rate.
  3. Repeat for upper wicks.

2) Important Tools & Indicators (How to set them)

Use these indicators and drawing tools together — they confirm each other.

Moving Averages (MA)

Set MA5 (fast), MA10 (medium), MA20 (trend). Use on 1-min & 5-min.

  • Price above MA20 & MAs slope up = Uptrend → bias = BUY (Green).
  • Price below MA20 & MAs slope down = Downtrend → bias = SELL (Red).

Bollinger Bands

Period 20, deviation 2. Use to detect volatility and reversal points.

RSI (14)

RSI <30 oversold → look for green reversal. RSI >70 overbought → look for red reversal.

MACD

MACD line crossing signal line confirms trend momentum change.

3) Drawing Tools — Trendlines, Support & Resistance, Channels

Draw these and reuse them — price respects old levels.

Trendlines

Connect at least 2 swing lows for an uptrend; 2 swing highs for downtrend. Third touch = strong validity.

  • When price bounces off an uptrendline — buy on bounce (look for lower wick).
  • Trendline break + retest → possible trend reversal.

Support & Resistance (S/R)

Use horizontal lines to mark areas where price reversed 2–4 times. Prefer zones instead of single lines.

  • Price touching support with lower wick = strong buy setup.
  • Price touching resistance with upper wick = strong sell setup.

4) Fibonacci Retracement — How to draw & use (Advanced)

Use in clear trends. Draw from trend swing low → swing high (for uptrend). Key levels: 23.6%, 38.2%, 50%, 61.8%, 78.6%.

  • Trade idea: In uptrend, wait for pullback to 50–61.8% — look for lower wick + bullish confirmation (MA bounce).
  • Stop: below next lower fib level or below local support.
  • Multiple timeframe: use 5-min for structure, 1-min for entries.

5) Strategies — 1-Minute Candle Strategy & Multi-Timeframe Strategy

Simple 1-Minute Strategy (Beginner)

Conditions for a GREEN entry:
  • Price near support line or Fibonacci 50–61.8% (on 5-min)
  • Lower wick on 1-min candle (≥2× body)
  • Price above MA20 on 1-min or MA20 sloping up
  • RSI < 40 then rising
Entry: Buy at next candle open.
Stop: Below wick low (2–3 pips / points).
Target: 1:1.5 or 1:2 Risk:Reward

Conditions for a RED entry: (mirror)

Advanced Strategy (Trend + Pullback + Fibonacci)

  1. Use 15-min or 5-min to identify trend (MA20 slope, higher highs / lower lows).
  2. On 1-min, wait for pullback to 50–61.8 fib that aligns with S/R zone.
  3. Confirm with a bullish candle showing long lower wick (≥3× body) and rising RSI.
  4. Enter on next candle. Tight stop below wick. Trail using MAs or previous swing.

Example (numbers)

Uptrend from 100 → 150. Retrace to 118 (61.8%). A 1-min candle shows very long lower wick and closes above MA5. RSI was 32 and now 38. Enter green next candle. Stop 2–3 points below wick bottom. Target 1:2 R:R.

6) Use indicator confirmation — Do not trade solely on wick

  • MA20 direction — primary trend filter.
  • RSI — divergence + oversold/overbought.
  • MACD — momentum confirmation (crosses).
  • Bollinger Bands — extremes & squeeze breakout.

If wick signal + MA trend + RSI momentum agree → probability increases substantially.

8) Risk Management & Psychology

  • Risk per trade: 0.5% – 2% of account. (Use fixed %)
  • Reward:risk minimum 1.5:1 (prefer 2:1).
  • Max consecutive losses: stop trading after 3 losing trades to reset emotions.
  • Keep a trade journal — record setup, entry, stop, outcome.

Money Management Example

If your account is ₹10,000 and you risk 1% → risk per trade = ₹100. If stop distance = 10 points, position size = ₹100/10 = ₹10 per point.

9) Quotex Withdrawal & Safety Tips (Short)

  • Withdraw to same method you deposited (UPI → UPI, bank → same bank).
  • Complete KYC before large withdrawals (Aadhaar, PAN, bank proof).
  • Don’t change IP frequently — avoid VPN during withdrawals.
  • Split large withdrawals (if allowed) — avoid many large same-day withdrawals that look suspicious.

These reduce the chance of holds and verification delays.

10) Advanced Tips & Tricks

  • Confluence is king: wick + S/R + MA + RSI + Fibonacci → much stronger signal.
  • Volume (if available): increase in volume on wick rejection = stronger signal.
  • Multiple timeframe alignment: 15-min trend + 5-min pullback + 1-min entry = best structure.
  • Trap patterns: small body & long wick near S/R often traps retail traders. Wait for confirmation before entry.
  • Backtest: record 100 wick setups and calculate accuracy before trusting any claim.
