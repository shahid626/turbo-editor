Quotex Wick Tricks — Colour HTML Guide

Copy these patterns to your 1-minute chart. Combine with MA20 / support-resistance and RSI for higher accuracy.

1. Long Lower Wick → Next candle mostly GREEN

Explanation: Price hit a low, buyers pushed it up and candle closed much higher than the wick bottom. Expect a green follow-up candle most of the time.

2. Long Upper Wick → Next candle mostly RED

Explanation: Price moved up but sellers rejected the highs, closing lower — expect red continuation or reversal.]

3. Double Bottom Wick (Two touches at same low) → Strong GREEN

Two wicks hitting the same low create a demand/support zone — higher probability of a strong green candle next.

4. Double Top Wick (Two touches at same high) → Strong RED

Two rejections at the same top create strong resistance — expect a significant red candle.

5. Wick Size Rule (2×, 3×, 4× body)

If wick is ≥2× the body, treat it as a high-probability reversal. Larger the ratio, stronger the signal.

6. Small Body + Long Wick = Smart-money trap

Often used to shake weak hands — the next candle typically moves opposite to the wick direction.

Cheat-sheet (Quick Rules)

• Long lower wick  → Next candle GREEN
• Long upper wick  → Next candle RED
• Double bottom     → Strong GREEN
• Double top        → Strong RED
• Wick ≥ 3× body    → Very high probability reversal
• At support + wick → Higher confidence (confirm with MA20/RSI)
Note: Combine with trend (MA20), support/resistance and RSI for best results. No method is 100%.
How to use: Look for these wick patterns near support/resistance or trendlines. Confirm with MA20 (direction) and RSI (overbought/oversold) for higher accuracy.
